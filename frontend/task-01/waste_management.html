Waste management is all about what we do with the things we throw away. Every day we use paper, plastic, food, and many other items. When we no longer need them, they can either become garbage or get a second life through recycling and reuse. If trash is just dumped, it can pollute the land, water, and air. But if we sort it properly, much of it can be used again. For example, paper can be recycled into new notebooks, bottles can be turned into new plastic items, and food scraps can become compost for gardens. A circular economy is like a magic loop where nothing is wasted. Instead of throwing things away, we keep using and reusing them. Imagine if your toy broke. Instead of tossing it, you fixed it or turned it into something new. That way, fewer resources are taken from the Earth. This helps save trees, reduce pollution, and protect animals. Kids can join in by reducing waste, reusing bottles and bags, recycling properly, and even making creative crafts out of old things. Just like superheroes, we can fight waste and keep our planet clean and happy.
